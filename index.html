Introduction

Setting the context for the study

🏘️

Dalimbari Gaon is a quaint and culturally rich village nestled in the West karbi anglong district of Assam. Like many villages in the region, it is a place where tradition and modernity exist side by side — where you can hear the evening prayers at the local church while also seeing the piped water connections from the Jal Jeevan Mission reaching households.

The village is home to a diverse population that includes indigenous communities, immigrant settlers, and people from various ethnic backgrounds. This diversity is reflected in the places of worship — a church and a Kali Mandir stand as symbols of religious harmony — and in the everyday lives of the people who celebrate each other's festivals with equal enthusiasm.

Economically, Dalimbari is primarily dependent on agriculture. Tea plantations, betel-nut cultivation, and paddy farming form the backbone of the local economy. However, like many rural areas in Assam, it faces its own set of challenges — limited healthcare infrastructure, the environmental impact of mining, and the need for better educational facilities.

This study was undertaken to understand these dynamics up close — to go beyond statistics and truly listen to the stories that the people and the landscape of Dalimbari have to tell. Through systematic fieldwork, personal interviews, and careful observation, we have attempted to paint an honest picture of where Dalimbari stands today and where it could head tomorrow.

Methodology

How the study was conducted

🔬

The research methodology adopted for this study was primarily qualitative and field-based. We believed that to truly understand a village like Dalimbari, we needed to walk its paths, sit in its courtyards, and listen to its people — not just fill out questionnaires from a distance.

Data Collection Methods:

📌 Personal Observation: Systematic observation of the village layout, infrastructure, economic activities, and social interactions during the field visit.

📌 Interviews: Informal and semi-structured interviews with villagers, local leaders, teachers, shopkeepers, and healthcare workers.

📌 Photographic Documentation: Photographs were taken at key locations — the church, Kali Mandir, schools, market, police station, river, agricultural fields, and more — to visually support the findings.

📌 Secondary Sources: Relevant government reports, census data, and published research on the region were consulted to supplement primary data.

The field visit was conducted over multiple days, allowing us to observe both weekday routines and weekend activities, giving us a well-rounded view of village life.

⛪ Church — The Heart of Faith

The church in Dalimbari is more than just a place of worship — it's the spiritual anchor of the Christian community in the village. On Sundays, the sound of hymns fills the air, drawing families together in prayer and fellowship. The church also plays a vital social role, organising community events, charity drives, and youth programmes that keep the younger generation connected to their roots.

The church standing tall as a symbol of faith and community
🛕 Kali Mandir — A Hindu Shrine

The Kali Mandir in Dalimbari is a significant place of worship for the Hindu community. Dedicated to Goddess Kali, the temple comes alive during festivals, especially Kali Puja, when the entire village — regardless of religion — participates in the celebrations. This beautiful coexistence of the church and the temple within the same village speaks volumes about the communal harmony that defines Dalimbari.

The entrance to the Kali Mandir

🏪 Market — The Economic Hub

The local market of Dalimbari is the economic pulse of the village. It's where farmers sell their produce, shopkeepers display everyday essentials, and the community gathers not just to trade but to exchange news and stories. The market operates on specific days of the week and attracts buyers and sellers from neighbouring villages as well.

Bustling market day in Dalimbari
⚽ Playground — Where the Young Dream

Every village needs a playground, and Dalimbari's open field serves as exactly that. From cricket matches to casual evening kickabouts, this space is where children learn teamwork, resolve conflicts, and simply enjoy being young. Investing in better sports infrastructure here could go a long way in nurturing talent and keeping youth engaged.

The village playground — where dreams take flight

Education is the cornerstone of any village's future, and Dalimbari is served by multiple schools that cater to different levels of learning. From primary schools that lay the foundation to higher-level institutions that prepare students for the world beyond, these schools represent hope and aspiration. 💧 Jal Jeevan Mission — Water for Every Home

The Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) is a flagship Central Government programme aimed at providing functional household tap connections to every rural household in India. In Dalimbari, many families now have access to clean drinking water right at their doorstep. However, challenges remain in terms of consistent water supply and maintenance of infrastructure.

Jal Jeevan Mission infrastructure in the village

🚔 Dalimbari Police Station

The Dalimbari Police Station serves as the law enforcement hub for the village and surrounding areas. It plays a crucial role in maintaining peace and order, resolving disputes, and ensuring the safety of residents. The station also serves as a point of contact for various government schemes and identity document services.

Dalimbari Police Station — front view
The station premises
🏥 Health Sub-Centre & Hospitality

Healthcare access is one of the most pressing concerns in rural Assam, and Dalimbari's sub-centre is the first point of medical contact for most residents. Staffed by an ANM and visiting doctors on specific days, the sub-centre handles basic treatments, maternal care, immunisation drives, and health awareness programmes. The lack of a full-time doctor means serious cases must be referred to the nearest town hospital.

The health sub-centre serving the village community
🌾 Tea, Agriculture & Betel-Nut Plantation

Agriculture is the lifeblood of Dalimbari's economy. The village is surrounded by lush green tea gardens that provide employment to many local families. Betel-nut plantations are another major source of income, while paddy cultivation during the monsoon ensures food security. Despite the hard work, the returns are often modest, and there is a pressing need for better irrigation and modern farming techniques.

Agricultural landscape — the backbone of Dalimbari's economy
⛏️ Mining & Deforestation

Perhaps the most concerning aspect of Dalimbari's development is the environmental degradation caused by mining and deforestation. Stone quarrying and sand mining have led to significant loss of forest cover, soil erosion, and disruption of local water bodies. While these activities provide short-term employment, the long-term ecological cost is staggering. Sustainable alternatives and stricter regulations are urgently needed.

Mining activity and its visible environmental impact
🏛️ Dalimbari First I.B.

The Dalimbari First Inspection Bungalow (I.B.) is a historic structure that has served as a government rest house for decades. Originally built during the British colonial era, it continues to accommodate travelling officials today. Its colonial-era architecture, though weathered by time, still carries a certain grandeur that makes it one of the notable landmarks of the village.

Dalimbari First I.B. — a piece of colonial-era heritage
🏞️ Naudubi River

The Naudubi River is one of the most important natural features of Dalimbari. It provides water for irrigation, supports local fishing, and serves as a recreational spot. However, the river is also prone to flooding during the monsoon, causing damage to crops and property. During our visit, the river was calm and picturesque — a stark contrast to the fury it can unleash during the rains.

The serene Naudubi River flowing through Dalimbari

Our analysis reveals that while Dalimbari has made meaningful progress in areas like water supply and primary education, significant gaps remain in healthcare, environmental conservation, and economic diversification. The village's strength lies in its community bonds and religious harmony, which provide a solid foundation for collective action towards development.

The dependence on agriculture makes the village economically vulnerable. Diversifying income sources — through skill development, small-scale industries, and eco-tourism — could significantly improve resilience.

Conclusion

Bringing it all together

🌟

Dalimbari Gaon is a village that beautifully embodies the contradictions and possibilities of rural India. It is a place where faith moves mountains — with a church and a temple standing as neighbours — but where clean water still doesn't reach every home. It is a place where children play with boundless joy in open fields, but where the same fields are slowly being eaten away by unchecked mining.

Our study has shown that the socio-economic development of Dalimbari is at a critical juncture. The village has the basic building blocks of progress — schools, a market, a police station, a health sub-centre, and a community that genuinely cares for one another. What it needs now is targeted investment, better governance, and a long-term vision that prioritises sustainability over short-term gains.

If the suggestions outlined in this report are taken seriously, there is every reason to believe that Dalimbari can transform from a village that is merely surviving to one that is truly thriving.

Suggestions

Practical recommendations for Dalimbari's development

  1. Strengthen Healthcare: Appoint a full-time doctor at the sub-centre and equip it with basic diagnostic facilities.
  2. Modernise Agriculture: Introduce modern farming techniques and establish cold storage facilities.
  3. Regulate Mining: Enforce strict environmental regulations and promote alternative livelihoods.
  4. Improve Educational Infrastructure: Upgrade school buildings and provide digital learning resources.
  5. Expand Jal Jeevan Mission: Ensure 100% coverage and establish a local maintenance committee.
  6. Promote Skill Development: Set up a skill training centre for tailoring, computer literacy, and handicrafts.
  7. Develop Eco-Tourism: Leverage the Naudubi River and surrounding landscapes responsibly.
  8. Flood Management: Invest in riverbank protection and early warning systems along the Naudubi.
  9. Enhance Connectivity: Improve road conditions and explore digital connectivity solutions.
  10. Empower Youth: Create a youth council and sports academy for constructive community engagement.
