1. Introduction

Setting the context for the study

🏘️

Dalimbari Gaon is a rural village located in the West Karbi Anglong district of Assam. The village is surrounded by hills, forests, agricultural land, and small water sources. The people of the village mainly depend on agriculture, daily wage labour, animal husbandry, and small businesses for their livelihood.

This field study was conducted to understand the social, economic, educational, cultural, and infrastructural condition of the village. The study also aimed to examine the challenges faced by the villagers and the role of government schemes in rural development.

3. Methodology

How the study was conducted

🔬

The methodology adopted for this field study was mainly qualitative and field-based. Direct observation and interaction with the villagers were used to collect information about the socio-economic condition of the village.

Methods of Data Collection:

a) Personal Observation: The village environment, housing condition, roads, agricultural activities, and social interactions were carefully observed during the field visit.

b) Interviews: Informal interviews were conducted with villagers, farmers, teachers, local leaders, shopkeepers, and youths to gather information about their lifestyle and problems.

c) Photographic Documentation: Photographs of important places such as schools, roads, religious places, markets, agricultural fields, and rivers were collected to support the study.

d) Secondary Sources: Information was also collected from census reports, government websites, books, newspapers, and online articles related to rural development and West Karbi Anglong district.

4. Demographic and Social Structure

Understanding the people and their way of life

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Dalimbari Gaon is mainly inhabited by tribal and rural communities. Most families live in small households and maintain close social relationships with one another. The villagers live peacefully and cooperate during festivals, agricultural work, and social functions.

Traditional customs and cultural practices are still preserved in the village. Elders are respected, and community participation plays an important role in maintaining social harmony.

5. Economic Activities

Livelihoods and income sources of the village

💰

Agriculture is the primary occupation of the people of Dalimbari Gaon. The major crops cultivated in the village include:

  • Rice
  • Maize
  • Vegetables
  • Ginger
  • Pulses

Many villagers also engage in:

  • Tea cultivation
  • Pig farming
  • Poultry farming
  • Daily wage labour
  • Small business activities

Women also contribute significantly to household income through farming, weaving, and livestock care.

However, the income level of many families remains low due to lack of modern agricultural technology, poor market access, and seasonal unemployment.

🏪 Village Market

The local market of Dalimbari is the economic pulse of the village. It's where farmers sell their produce, shopkeepers display everyday essentials, and the community gathers not just to trade but to exchange news and stories. The market attracts buyers and sellers from neighbouring villages as well, making it a microcosm of the rural economy in action.

Bustling market day in Dalimbari
🌾 Tea, Agriculture & Betel-Nut Plantation

The village is surrounded by lush green tea gardens that provide employment to many local families. Betel-nut (areca nut) plantations are another major source of income, with the tall, slender areca palms forming a defining feature of the landscape. Paddy cultivation during the monsoon season ensures food security, while vegetable gardens supplement both nutrition and income.

Agricultural landscape — the backbone of Dalimbari's economy

6. Educational Condition

The state of learning and literacy in the village

📚

Educational awareness in the village is gradually improving. Primary schools are available nearby, and some students travel to nearby towns for higher education.

Teachers and parents are encouraging children to continue their studies. However, several educational problems still exist:

  • Lack of higher educational institutions
  • Financial difficulties among poor families
  • Transportation problems
  • Limited access to digital learning facilities

Despite these challenges, the literacy rate is slowly increasing.

🏫 Educational Institutions of Dalimbari

Dalimbari is served by multiple schools that cater to different levels of learning. From primary schools that lay the foundation to higher-level institutions that prepare students for the world beyond, these schools represent hope and aspiration. 7. Infrastructure and Public Facilities

Roads, electricity, water, healthcare, and public institutions

🛤️ Roads and Transportation

Road connectivity has improved in recent years, but some roads become muddy and difficult during the rainy season. Transportation remains a challenge, especially for students and patients who need to travel to nearby towns.

💡 Electricity

Most households have electricity connections, though power cuts are still common in some areas. The availability of electricity has improved the quality of life, but reliability remains an issue.

🚰 Water Supply

People mainly depend on tube wells, hand pumps, and natural water sources for drinking water. The Jal Jeevan Mission has started making an impact by providing piped water connections to households, as seen in the images below.

Jal Jeevan Mission infrastructure in the village
Water supply connections reaching households
🏥 Healthcare

Healthcare facilities are limited. Villagers often travel to nearby towns or health centres for medical treatment. The health sub-centre is the first point of medical contact for most residents, staffed by an ANM and visiting doctors on specific days.

The health sub-centre serving the village community
🚔 Dalimbari Police Station

The Dalimbari Police Station serves as the law enforcement hub for the village and surrounding areas. It plays a crucial role in maintaining peace and order, resolving disputes, and ensuring the safety of residents. The station also serves as a point of contact for various government schemes and identity document services.

Dalimbari Police Station — front view
The station premises
🏛️ Dalimbari First I.B.

The Dalimbari First Inspection Bungalow (I.B.) is a historic structure that has served as a government rest house for decades. Originally built during the British colonial era, it continues to accommodate travelling officials today. Its colonial-era architecture, though weathered by time, still carries a certain grandeur that makes it one of the notable landmarks of the village.

8. Government Schemes and Rural Development

The role of government programmes in village development

🏛️

Government schemes have contributed to the development of Dalimbari Gaon. Some important schemes benefiting the villagers include:

  • MGNREGA — providing employment guarantee to rural households
  • PMAY (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana) — improving housing conditions
  • Jal Jeevan Mission — providing piped drinking water to households
  • Rural road development schemes — improving connectivity

These programmes have helped improve housing conditions, employment opportunities, drinking water facilities, and infrastructure development. However, the reach and effectiveness of these schemes vary, and many villagers feel that more needs to be done.

9. Cultural and Religious Life

Traditions, faith, and community bonding

🛐

The people of Dalimbari Gaon actively participate in traditional and religious festivals. Cultural programmes, community feasts, and traditional dances are important parts of village life.

Religious institutions and social gatherings help strengthen unity and cooperation among the villagers. The coexistence of a church and a Kali Mandir within the same village is a beautiful testament to the communal harmony that defines Dalimbari.

⛪ Church — The Heart of Faith

The church in Dalimbari is more than just a place of worship — it's the spiritual anchor of the Christian community in the village. On Sundays, the sound of hymns fills the air, drawing families together in prayer and fellowship. The church also plays a vital social role, organising community events, charity drives, and youth programmes.

The church standing tall as a symbol of faith and community
🛕 Kali Mandir — A Hindu Shrine

The Kali Mandir in Dalimbari is a significant place of worship for the Hindu community. Dedicated to Goddess Kali, the temple comes alive during festivals, especially Kali Puja, when the entire village — regardless of religion — participates in the celebrations.

10. Environmental Challenges

Threats to the natural environment and village sustainability

🌍

The village faces several environmental and developmental challenges such as:

  • Deforestation
  • Soil erosion
  • Poor road condition during monsoon
  • Lack of employment opportunities
  • Limited healthcare facilities
  • Poverty among some households

These challenges affect the overall socio-economic development of the village.

⛏️ Mining & Deforestation

Stone quarrying and sand mining in the region have led to significant loss of forest cover, soil erosion, and disruption of local water bodies. While these activities provide short-term employment and revenue, the long-term ecological cost is staggering. Residents expressed growing concern about the diminishing green cover and the increasing frequency of floods. Sustainable alternatives and stricter regulations are urgently needed.

Mining activity and its visible environmental impact
🏞️ Naudubi River

The Naudubi River is one of the most important natural features of Dalimbari. It provides water for irrigation, supports local fishing, and serves as a recreational spot. However, like many rivers in Assam, the Naudubi is also prone to flooding during the monsoon season, causing damage to crops and property. During our visit, the river was calm and picturesque — a stark contrast to the fury it can unleash during the rains.

The serene Naudubi River flowing through Dalimbari

12. Suggestions

Measures to improve the socio-economic condition of Dalimbari Gaon

  1. Improvement of road and transportation facilities — so that villagers can access markets, schools, and hospitals more easily throughout the year.
  2. Establishment of better healthcare centres — with full-time doctors and basic diagnostic equipment to reduce dependence on distant town hospitals.
  3. Promotion of modern agricultural techniques — to increase productivity and reduce the physical burden on farmers.
  4. Development of skill training programmes for youth — in areas like computer literacy, tailoring, food processing, and handicrafts to diversify income sources.
  5. Better educational facilities and digital access — upgrading school infrastructure and providing digital learning tools to bridge the urban-rural education gap.
  6. Increased employment opportunities through rural industries — promoting small-scale and cottage industries that leverage local resources and skills.
  7. Environmental protection and afforestation programmes — to counter the damage caused by mining and deforestation and ensure long-term ecological sustainability.

13. Conclusion

Bringing it all together

🌟

Dalimbari Gaon is gradually progressing in terms of education, infrastructure, and rural development. Agriculture continues to be the main source of livelihood, while government schemes have positively impacted the village economy.

However, challenges such as unemployment, poor healthcare, and environmental problems still remain. With proper planning, awareness, and continued government support, Dalimbari Gaon can achieve sustainable socio-economic development in the future.

The village has the basic building blocks of progress — schools, a market, a police station, a health sub-centre, and a community that genuinely cares for one another. What it needs now is targeted investment, better governance, and a long-term vision that prioritises sustainability over short-term gains. The people of Dalimbari deserve nothing less.
